FAULTY PLUG FIRES

“Never use square box plug adapters, and if using a strip adapter/extension make sure it’s fused and don’t overload it."

Most electrical fires are caused by faulty electrical outlets or worn out sockets that aren't properly grounded. With age, the wiring behind sockets and outlets wears, the wires loosen over time and can eventually break and cause a fire. -Fire Seals Direct

Grease fires happen when oil, grease, or fat on a stovetop, oven, or deep-fat fryer gets hot enough to ignite. These fires burn very hot and can spread if not handled properly.

If this happens to you: Turn off the stove. Cover the pot, pan, or fryer with a lid. Douse the fire with baking soda, salt, or a fire extinguisher—never water. -Verywell Health

CANDLE FIRES

There’s a special beauty and tranquility to candles, but a lighted candle is also an open flame, and a potential fire hazard if not carefully monitored. In fact, an estimated 8,200 home fires are started by candles each year according to the National Fire Protection Association. -National Candle Association

NEVER leave a lighted candle unattended especially if it is near curtains or anything flammable.
